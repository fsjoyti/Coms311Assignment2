id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Complex system</h1>									<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>												<div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">				</div><p>A <b>complex system</b> is a <a href="/wiki/System" title="System">system</a> composed of many components which may interact with each other. In many cases it is useful to represent such a system as a network where the nodes represent the components and the links their interactions. Examples of complex systems are Earth's global <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Organisms" class="mw-redirect" title="Organisms">organisms</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Human_brain" title="Human brain">human brain</a>, social organization, an <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>, a living <a href="/wiki/Cell_(biology)" title="Cell (biology)">cell</a>, and ultimately the entire <a href="/wiki/Universe" title="Universe">universe</a>.</p><div id="toc" class="toc"><h2>Contents</h2><ul><li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-2"><a class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Complex_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>In the area of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, arguably the largest contribution to the study of complex systems was the discovery of <a href="/wiki/Chaos_theory" title="Chaos theory">chaos</a> in <a href="/wiki/Deterministic" class="mw-redirect" title="Deterministic">deterministic</a> systems, a feature of certain <a href="/wiki/Dynamical_systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Dynamical systems">dynamical systems</a> that is strongly related to <a href="/wiki/Nonlinearity" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonlinearity">nonlinearity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The study of <a href="/wiki/Neural_networks" class="mw-redirect" title="Neural networks">neural networks</a> was also integral in advancing the mathematics needed to study complex systems.</p><p>The first research institute focused on complex systems, the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_Institute" title="Santa Fe Institute">Santa Fe Institute</a>, was founded in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Early Santa Fe Institute participants included physics Nobel laureates <a href="/wiki/Murray_Gell-Mann" title="Murray Gell-Mann">Murray Gell-Mann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philip_Warren_Anderson" title="Philip Warren Anderson">Philip Anderson</a>, economics Nobel laureate <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Arrow" title="Kenneth Arrow">Kenneth Arrow</a>, and Manhattan Project scientists <a href="/wiki/George_Cowan" title="George Cowan">George Cowan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Herbert_L._Anderson" title="Herbert L. Anderson">Herb Anderson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Today, there are over 50 <a href="/wiki/Complex_systems#Institutes_and_research_centers" title="Complex systems">institutes and research centers</a> focusing on complex systems.</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nonlinear_systems">Nonlinear systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Complex_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nonlinear systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3><h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Chaotic_systems">Chaotic systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Complex_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chaotic systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbcaption">Assign <i>z</i> to <i>z</i><sup>2</sup> minus the conjugate of <i>z</i>, plus the original value of the pixel for each pixel, colouring pixels per the number iterations until the absolute value of <i>z</i> exceeds two; display using inversion (borders are inner set), so that you can see that it<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. Thus, an arbitrarily small perturbation of the current trajectory may lead to significantly different future behavior.</p><p><a href="/wiki/Complex_adaptive_system" title="Complex adaptive system">Complex adaptive systems</a> (CAS) are special cases of complex systems. They are <a href="/wiki/Complexity" title="Complexity">complex</a> in that they are diverse and made up of multiple interconnected elements and <a href="/wiki/Adaptive" class="mw-redirect" title="Adaptive">adaptive</a> in that they have the capacity to change and learn from experience. Examples of complex adaptive systems include the <a href="/wiki/Stock_market" title="Stock market">stock market</a>, social insect and <a href="/wiki/Ant" title="Ant">ant</a> colonies, the <a href="/wiki/Biosphere" title="Biosphere">biosphere</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Human_brain" title="Human brain">brain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune system</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cell_(biology)" title="Cell (biology)">cell</a> and the developing <a href="/wiki/Embryo" title="Embryo">embryo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manufacturing" title="Manufacturing">manufacturing businesses</a> and any human social group-based endeavor in a cultural and <a href="/wiki/Social_system" title="Social system">social system</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a> or <a href="/wiki/Community" title="Community">communities</a>. This includes some large-scale online systems, such as collaborative tagging or <a href="/wiki/Social_bookmarking" title="Social bookmarking">social bookmarking</a> systems.</p><p>Complex systems may
